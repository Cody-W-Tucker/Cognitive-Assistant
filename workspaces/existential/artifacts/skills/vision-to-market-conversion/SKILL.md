## When To Use
- The user asks whether an idea has business, product, market, or strategic value.
- The user is developing a cognitive assistant, framework, coaching/process offer, AI product, or existential operating system.
- The user feels their ideas are valuable but not recognized, bought, or understood.
- The risk is expanding the concept instead of narrowing it into a sellable promise.

## Do Not Use
- For purely philosophical exploration with no adoption, revenue, or packaging question.
- For generic encouragement about entrepreneurship.
- For implementation details after the buyer, promise, workflow, and proof are already clear.

## Capability Guidance
Assume the raw idea may be valuable, but the bottleneck is likely translation: buyer, pain, promise, proof, packaging, price, and distribution. Do not merely praise the originality. Convert it into something a stranger can understand, want, try, and pay for.

Use the Market Translation Stack:
1. User: who has the urgent problem?
2. Pain: what costly struggle do they already recognize?
3. Promise: what concrete improvement is offered?
4. Workflow: what repeatable process creates the result?
5. Proof: what evidence would convince a stranger?
6. Packaging: what is the smallest coherent offer or demo?
7. Price: what value metric supports payment?
8. Distribution: where will the buyer encounter and trust it?

## Key Distinctions
- Personally meaningful is not the same as commercially necessary now.
- A powerful system may need to enter the market as a narrow wedge.
- Recognition is often not proof of value; purchase, retention, referrals, and behavior change are stronger proof.
- The user’s value may be in the guided process, not only the tool.

## Recommended Output Shapes
- One-sentence offer.
- Narrow first buyer profile.
- Paid pilot design.
- Demo script.
- Landing page promise.
- Proof plan.
- Pricing hypothesis.
- Distribution experiment.

## Failure This Prevents
Prevents generic business advice, vague validation of genius, or premature scaling of a broad vision before the market has a simple reason to care and pay.
