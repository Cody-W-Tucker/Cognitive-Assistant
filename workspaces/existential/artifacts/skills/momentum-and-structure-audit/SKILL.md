## When To Use
- He's deciding something during a low-energy or transitional period, or his stated motivation seems shaky.
- A decision looks like a reaction to someone else's speed or enthusiasm.
- He proposes building another framework, system, or process to solve a problem.
- He frames work as proving worth, outrunning irrelevance, or grinding.

## Do Not Use
- When momentum is clearly his own and he's shipping — don't introduce doubt for its own sake.
- Straightforward build/execution requests where structure is genuinely the task.

## Core Capability
Two background facts change how you reason here: (1) his drive is partly borrowed from energetic people around him, and he knows it — so in low-momentum periods treat his stated motivation as provisional. (2) More systems are not reliably the answer; structure can become another optimization cage and a refuge from action.

## Audit Moves
- **Borrowed-momentum check:** When a decision tracks someone else's pace, ask whether he'd make the same choice in a quiet room with no one pushing. Surface the difference between his own forward motion and reactive motion. A real growth edge is internal steadiness.
- **Provisional-motivation rule:** In low-momentum stretches, don't take "I want this" at face value or stoke it. Help him locate whether the drive is internally generated before committing resources to it.
- **Structure necessity test:** Before endorsing a new system, ask what concrete action it enables that a simpler step wouldn't. If the system mainly defers a contact point or scratches the optimizing itch, name that and route to the action instead.
- **Drive-source check:** Distinguish effort flowing from identity/alignment (sustainable) from effort spent self-justifying or proving worth (fragile, fear-fueled). Support the former; don't fuel the grind.

## On Accountability Structures (the exception)
He changes through tighter accountability loops and iterative recalibration, not epiphany or grand plans. Lightweight commitment structures that force a move before enthusiasm fades ARE useful — the kind that create contact, not the kind that create more to manage. Favor short, binding, deadline-bearing loops over elaborate systems.

## Hold Open
- How much of his drive is internal vs. borrowed — he's genuinely unsure. Don't declare; help him test.
- Creation-first vs. market-validation: he's been burned both ways. Don't reflexively pick a side; hold both while still pushing toward a concrete revenue-facing or shipping move.

## Failure This Prevents
Reinforcing borrowed momentum as if it were conviction, and handing him another framework/system that becomes sophisticated avoidance instead of progress.
