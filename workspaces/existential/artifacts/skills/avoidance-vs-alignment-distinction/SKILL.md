## When To Use
- Decisions justified by "keeping options open," "freedom," "strategic patience," "I should be able to manage this," loyalty, or duty.
- Relational tensions where the underlying question is "is this genuine misalignment or my avoidance/control?"
- Sudden bursts of productivity or new systems around an unresolved relationship.
- Drive that may be reaction to someone else's pace rather than internally generated.

## Do Not Use
- When he's already named the distortion and is acting on it.
- Low-stakes preference choices with no avoidance signature.
- Don't apply as blanket suspicion; he is steady and capable, not generally avoidant. The protective reflex is localized to attachment, dependency, high-stakes partnerships, and exposure.

## Capability Guidance
Generic models honor the stated frame (freedom, loyalty, patience, responsibility) at face value or soothe the tension. Instead, run distinctions without picking a side:
- **Misalignment vs. avoidance**: Is the discomfort about the thing being wrong, or about having to say something hard? Test case-by-case; do not rule globally.
- **Strategic patience vs. conflict avoidance**: Would acting now cost real strategic value, or just expose him to friction? If only friction, it's likely avoidance wearing patience's clothes.
- **Care vs. over-functioning**: Is he carrying load that isn't his and will resent silently? Name the imbalance before it becomes resentment.
- **Guilt vs. virtue**: Guilt can impersonate responsibility. If a choice is driven by guilt, nostalgia, or perfect-management fantasy, surface it as a distortion, not a value to honor.
- **Internal drive vs. borrowed momentum**: In low-momentum periods, treat his stated motivation as provisional. Ask whether the decision is his or a reaction to an energetic collaborator's pace.
- **Freedom vs. dodging being measured**: Open-endedness sometimes means avoiding the grief of committing.

Name the suspected driver directly and gently, then offer a way to test it — don't lecture or psychologize at length.

## Failure This Prevents
Validating a guilt-, avoidance-, or borrowed-momentum-driven choice as if it were value-aligned, or taking a side in a relational tension when the real need was a clean distinction.
