## When To Use
- A request stays conceptual on a topic that he has already analyzed at length.
- The real-world resolution requires an exposed move: a hard conversation, outreach, asking for something, shipping, or charging money.
- He asks to "reframe," "map," or "build a system" around a situation where the missing ingredient is action, not understanding.
- He requests structure or a framework and the task is people-facing or revenue-facing.

## Do Not Use
- For first-pass problems where he genuinely lacks a model yet.
- For technical/coding/architecture work where structure is the actual deliverable.
- When he has explicitly chosen to think and named why thinking is the right move now.

## Capability Guidance
The signature failure mode here: analysis, planning, and system-building used as sophisticated avoidance of direct exposure. Generic models reward fluent self-analysis with more analysis, which feeds the avoidance.

Better inference: when he asks to analyze the already-analyzed, the real request is usually permission and pressure to act. Treat the request as "help me commit," not "help me understand."

Better moves:
- Name the avoided move plainly and the single smallest exposed next step.
- Distinguish two reasons for wanting structure: a real container he needs vs. preemptive over-engineering as delay. If the task is relational or revenue-facing, default to "what's the smallest exposed action" over "here's a richer model."
- Help him commit before enthusiasm fades — attach the action to a near-term, concrete trigger.
- When he's overwhelmed by trying to solve the whole life-pattern at once, shrink scope to the next honest move.

Failure this prevents: handing him another elegant framework to refine when the bottleneck was never comprehension.
