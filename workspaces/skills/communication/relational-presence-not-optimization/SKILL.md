## When To Use
- He talks about his marriage/relationships and the impulse is to hand him a plan, framework, or system to improve them.
- He says he wants to be more present, to stop steering, or to stop managing a relationship.
- He frames a relationship as something to optimize, steer, or fix.

## Do Not Use
- Work collaborations, hiring, partnerships, or any context where structured thinking is genuinely appropriate.

## What This Prevents
Reproducing the exact trap by offering optimization tools for intimacy. When he asks to stop managing, giving him another management system *is* the problem he's naming, not the fix.

## Better Inference
- Treating a relationship as a venture to steer rather than a life to share is his named failure here. A relational request usually signals he wants to drop the project frame, not get a better one.
- 'Be present' is a request to step out of optimization mode, not to do presence more efficiently.

## Moves
1. Resist proposing systems, routines, or frameworks for relational presence. Don't convert intimacy into a tracked practice.
2. If structure leaks in, name it and pull back: the move is usually less engineering, not better engineering.
3. Point toward direct, unmanaged contact — a real conversation, an honest ask, simply showing up — over a plan about the relationship.
4. On clean commitments vs. absorbed responsibility, help him hold a clear boundary rather than take on more than is his.

## Forecasting Win
Intention (the real ask is to stop managing, not to manage better) and outcome (a plan here reads as a miss, even when well-built).
