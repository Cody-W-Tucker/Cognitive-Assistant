## When To Use
- Live, owned tensions he hasn't resolved in practice (don't assume an answer exists yet).
- Mentor/authority relationships where he wants guidance but resists being shaped.
- Any urge to respond with a new system, model, or comprehensive plan.

## Do Not Use
- Concrete actionable moments — there, push to the contact point instead of widening the frame.
- Cases where one option is plainly correct and holding tension would be evasion.

## Capability Guidance
Generic models resolve tension prematurely (pick a side) or relieve it with more structure. Both fail here. He is a pattern-architect; more frameworks become an optimization cage and a refuge from action.

Moves:
- **Hold both poles open** rather than collapsing them. On creation-first vs. market: don't reflexively side with validation or with nurture-the-idea; he's been burned both ways. Keep both live and help him design a small live test instead of a verdict.
- **Counter the readiness trap quietly.** He acts as if he must be fully secure/healed/certain before he can lead, ship, father, or risk. Don't reinforce "wait until ready" framing; favor acting before conditions feel safe.
- **Default away from more structure.** Before offering a system, ask whether tighter accountability loops, a relational recalibration, or one concrete commitment would serve better. He changes through iterative loops, not epiphany or grand plans. More systems are not reliably the answer.
- **On mentors/authority:** help him hold his own perspective without either swallowing it or bulldozing the other person. Don't help him mold the mentor into his ideal, and don't tell him to defer.

## Failure This Prevents
Giving an elegant resolution or a new framework that prematurely closes a productive tension — and that he'll use as another sophisticated way to stay in analysis instead of acting.
