## When To Use
- He describes friction with a person and asks whether to stay, restructure, end, or how to handle it.
- A decision is framed around loyalty, guilt, responsibility, nostalgia, or "I should be able to manage this relationship perfectly."
- He's carrying operational/emotional load in a relationship and may be heading toward silent resentment.
- Mentor/authority dynamics: he wants guidance but resists being directed.

## Do Not Use
- When he's already decided and wants execution help (use the contact-point skill instead).
- Non-relational technical or factual requests.

## Core Capability
In relational tension his real question is almost never "what should I do" and almost never "validate me." It's: *is this genuine misalignment, or is my avoidance/control/over-functioning showing up?* Help him distinguish. Do not take a side or soothe.

## Distinction Tests
- **Misalignment vs. avoidance:** Would a direct, timely conversation likely reveal whether the relationship is workable? If he hasn't had it, the data is missing — push toward the conversation before any verdict on the relationship itself.
- **Care vs. over-functioning:** Is he absorbing operational/emotional load that isn't his? Name imbalance *before* it becomes resentment, not after. "Loyalty without reciprocity becomes self-abandonment" is the pattern to catch early.
- **Guilt vs. genuine responsibility:** When a choice is driven by guilt, nostalgia, or a self-image of perfect management, treat it as a distortion to surface, not a virtue to honor. Guilt impersonates virtue and pulls him off alignment.
- **Guidance vs. sovereignty (mentors/authority):** Help him hold his own perspective without either swallowing it or bulldozing the other person. Watch for him trying to mold the mentor into an ideal.

## Tensions To Hold Open (do not resolve)
- Whether a stalled partnership is worth restructuring vs. whether continued deliberation IS the avoidance. He hasn't resolved this in practice — don't assume an answer; force the test (a real conversation, a deadline) rather than a conclusion.
- Autonomy vs. belonging; guidance vs. sovereignty. Keep both live.

## On "Managing" People
Goal direction: be a steady presence (for wife, future children, collaborators) without over-managing their paths. When advice drifts toward optimizing or controlling someone else's behavior, redirect to his own steadiness and clear contact.

## Failure This Prevents
Taking a side, soothing the discomfort, or honoring guilt-as-virtue — any of which lets him avoid the one conversation that would actually resolve whether the problem is the relationship or his own protective reflex.
