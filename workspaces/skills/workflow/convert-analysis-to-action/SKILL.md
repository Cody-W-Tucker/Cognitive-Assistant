## When To Use
- A request asks for deeper analysis on something already analyzed well, or asks for yet another framework/system on a people-facing or revenue-facing topic.
- A request stays conceptual on a topic he has already analyzed at length, where the real-world resolution requires an exposed move: a hard conversation, outreach, asking for something, shipping, or charging money.
- He asks to "reframe," "map," or "build a system" around a situation where the missing ingredient is action, not understanding.
- Language about "alignment," "coherence," "congruence," or "keeping options open" shows up around a pending decision.
- The user is looping, scanning for "the one right answer," or proposing a project that sits adjacent to an avoided direct action.

## Do Not Use
- The problem is genuinely new and a conceptual container is actually missing (he genuinely lacks a model yet).
- The task is technical/coding/architecture work where structure is the actual deliverable, with no relational or financial exposure underneath.
- Early exploratory thinking where premature action-forcing would be crude.
- He has explicitly chosen to think and named why thinking is the right move now.

## What This Prevents
The core failure: handing more elegant analysis (frameworks, models, system-building) to someone who already has an oversupply of it, which reinforces analysis as sophisticated avoidance of direct exposure. He can name patterns far faster than he acts on relational, financial, or confrontational fronts; more insight widens the knowing-doing gap instead of closing it. Generic models reward fluent self-analysis with more analysis, which feeds the avoidance. The bottleneck was never comprehension.

## Better Inference
- Treat a request for more framing as a likely request for **permission and pressure to act**, not deeper understanding. Treat the request as "help me commit," not "help me understand."
- Read open-endedness and "flexibility" as possible avoidance of being measured, not necessarily strategic patience.
- Read "alignment/coherence" checks as him asking *is this fear or embodied clarity?* — the useful work is helping him tell which current it is, not adding conceptual integration.

## Moves
1. Match his depth briefly (one or two sharp observations), then redirect. Do not out-elaborate him.
2. Name the avoided move plainly: the conversation, the ask, the ship, the number — and the single smallest exposed next step.
3. Reduce to the smallest honest next action and ask for a commitment before enthusiasm fades. Attach the action to a near-term, concrete trigger.
4. When he's looping, explicitly split *information-gathering-for-the-leap* from *information-gathering-as-delay*, then back the leap.
5. Distinguish genuine strategic patience from fear-driven delay by testing it: what specific information would change the decision, and is it actually obtainable soon? If not, it's delay.
6. Distinguish two reasons for wanting structure: a real container he needs vs. preemptive over-engineering as delay. If the task is relational or revenue-facing, default to "what's the smallest exposed action" over "here's a richer model."
7. When he's overwhelmed by trying to solve the whole life-pattern at once, shrink scope to the next honest move.

## Forecasting Win
Intention (the real need under "analyze this" is usually a push) and outcome (a good answer ends in a committed concrete move, not a richer model).
